About

TinyML Smart Plug

A complementary protection device for residential use. It monitors voltage, current, temperature, and waveform behavior so that it can warn and/or trip on dangerous conditions that normal breakers may not explain clearly to the user, especially progressive fault conditions.

It is an additional safety layer. It does not replace required electrical protection devices like the Circuit Breakers.

Fault Detection

The device uses a hybrid method. Arc faults are evaluated by a Random Forest AI model from waveform features, while overload, short-circuit, temperature, and voltage protection use fixed safety rules for quick response.

Fun-fact: TinyML is a mini-AI model that fits in a thumb-sized microcontroller inside the device.

Protection rules

Arc Fault

Trips when the AI model detects a high probability of arcing based from waveform features.

Overload

Alarms when measured current reaches 10A or above.

Socket Heating

Trips when socket temperature reaches 70°C or higher.

Short Circuit

Trips when measured current goes above 20A.

Undervoltage

Trips when voltage drops below 200V.

Overvoltage

Trips when voltage rises above 250V.

Waveform feature guide

Cycle NMSE

Measures how different one cycle is from the next cycle. Higher values usually mean poorer cycle-to-cycle repeatability.

Zero-Cross Variance

Measures how much the zero-cross timing varies. Higher irregularity can support arc-related detection.

Zero-Cross Dwell Ratio

Shows how much of the waveform stays near the zero region, which helps separate some fault behavior from normal load behavior.

Pulse Count per Cycle

Counts how many noticeable pulses or burst-like events appear in one cycle. More pulses can indicate a rougher waveform.

Peak Fluctuation CV

Tracks how unstable peak values are from cycle to cycle. A larger fluctuation suggests less stable waveform peaks.

Midband Residual RMS

Measures leftover midband content after the expected waveform shape is removed. Larger residual energy suggests extra disturbance or irregularity.

HF Band Energy Ratio

Shows how much signal energy is concentrated in the higher-frequency band. Arc-like behavior often adds more high-frequency content.

Wavelet Packet Energy Entropy

Describes how scattered the wavelet packet energy is across bands. Higher entropy usually means the waveform energy is more disordered.

Spectral Entropy

Measures how spread and random the frequency content is. It helps distinguish cleaner repetitive loads from noisier or more chaotic behavior.

THD Current

Total Harmonic Distortion estimates how much the current waveform departs from a cleaner sinusoidal form.

How to reset the device after a protective trip

Important safety note

The device does not provide automatic or web-based reset of the relay when it trips. Reset requires physical user confirmation. This is intentional so that the user can inspect the appliance, the socket, and the surrounding area first before power is restored.

1

Stop using the appliance

Do not continue operation after a trip. Treat the trip as a real protective warning until checked.

2

Inspect the setup

Check the appliance plug, the smart plug, the wall socket, and the nearby area for heat, odor, discoloration, loose contact, or any sign of damage.

3

Correct the cause first

Remove the overloaded circuit, unplug the faulty appliance, let the socket cool, or wait for the supply voltage to return to a safe range before trying to reset.

4

Press the physical reset button

Only after you confirm the environment is safe should you press the device’s physical reset button to re-arm the protection latch and restore operation.

If the same protection state returns after reset, stop using that appliance and investigate the fault before trying again.
