Purpose

What this device is for

The TinyML Smart Plug is a complementary protection device designed to help catch fire-inducing electrical faults earlier by combining sensing, deterministic protection logic, and TinyML inference.

Sensor-driven fault awareness with load context

It monitors voltage, current, and temperature, then uses TinyML Tree Ensemble models together with a Load Family classifier to distinguish normal appliance behavior from patterns that can point to hazardous arc-like or abnormal operating conditions.
Complementary protection It adds another protective layer. It is meant to assist, not replace, safe wiring, breakers, proper outlets, and careful appliance use.
Context-aware logic The load family classifier helps the system interpret the same waveform differently across resistive, motor, SMPS, and other load behaviors.
Action-oriented dashboard The website exists to show live state, explain the current condition, and let you respond safely when a trip or warning occurs.
Detection stack
Sensors + rules + TinyML
Fast protection logic and model inference work together.
Main job
Catch unsafe behavior early
Especially faults that can heat, overload, destabilize mains, or resemble arc-related events.
Best use
Monitor, verify, respond
Read the state, remove the cause, then reset only after the setup is safe again.
What the dashboard tells you

Current operating state

Whether the device is healthy, unplugged, stale, disconnected, warning, or tripped by a protective event.

Why the models matter

Tree ensembles can stay lightweight

The design uses TinyML Tree Ensemble models because they are practical for embedded inference while still handling nonlinear feature thresholds.

Protection

What can warn or trip the device

The exact firmware logic can evolve, but these are the protection classes the guide should communicate clearly and cleanly.
Arc-related abnormality

Arc Fault

Detected from waveform features, context, and model behavior rather than from a single simple threshold. This is one of the main reasons the device exists.

High current

Overload

Used for warning or protective action when the connected load draws more current than the intended operating envelope.

Thermal safety

Socket Heating

Temperature is tracked continuously. Rising heat can indicate loose contacts, outlet stress, or overload conditions even when current alone looks acceptable.

Mains quality

Under/Overvoltage

The dashboard separates unhealthy mains from full mains loss so the UI can distinguish poor supply conditions from a truly unplugged or absent-voltage state.

Unplugged is not the same as undervoltage

Very low measured voltage is treated separately so the device can clearly show when mains are effectively absent instead of just weak.

Warnings still matter

Even when the unit has not latched into a full trip, repeated warnings are valuable because they may show a problem that is growing worse over time.

Safe response

What to do after a fault

Remove the cause first, then reset only when the setup looks safe again.
Before resetting

Immediate response

  • Read the fault shown on the dashboard.
  • Inspect the appliance, plug, cord, socket, and nearby area.
  • Remove or correct the likely cause first.
After resetting

What to watch next

  • Reconnect only when the condition seems safe.
  • Watch the banner and overview again.
  • If the same fault returns, stop using the appliance and inspect more deeply.
Arc fault

Stop using the appliance, inspect for looseness, burning smell, carbonization, damaged cords, or intermittent contact. Do not keep re-testing by repeated reconnects.

Overload or sustained overload

Reduce the connected load, check whether the appliance demand is appropriate for the plug and outlet, then retry only after the load is brought down.

Socket heating

Check the outlet physically. Heat can point to poor contact, outlet wear, or excessive sustained stress even before obvious burning is visible.

Undervoltage or overvoltage

Wait for mains to stabilize. Do not treat abnormal supply as a device bug until the incoming voltage has clearly recovered.

General rule: never treat a protection trip as something to bypass. Remove the cause first, then reset only after you have confirmed the appliance and outlet are safe.
